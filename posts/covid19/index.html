COVID-19 Time Series Plots

Pascal Getreuer

Here are plots as of 2020-03-16 of active cases of COVID-19 (coronavirus disease 2019) over time, including presumptive positive cases. The data is from this github repo maintained by Johns Hopkins University Center for Systems Science and Engineering.

The dotted lines are 3-day predictions made by regressing an exponential fit over data from the last 7 days. For instance for the US, the regression is

\[ \text{active cases} = 4522 \cdot 2^{t / 2.7\text{ days}}, \]

predicting that active US cases will double in 2.7 days.

But we can slow it down by taking precautions. How to protect yourself (US CDC).
